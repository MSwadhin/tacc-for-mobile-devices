\section{Future Work}

Several extensions would strengthen the present study. The first priority is evaluation on the raw Dartmouth/IEEE DataPort WLAN archive or another public trace with comparable AP-level association logs. The current trace-compatible generator preserves the pipeline and creates meaningful handoff structure, but raw mobility traces would allow stronger claims about deployment magnitude, temporal stationarity, and AP heterogeneity.

The second priority is temporal evaluation. The present experiment treats perturbation rate as a controlled operating condition. A stronger online study would split mobility into time windows, estimate the handoff graph and demand model from past windows, deploy a cache placement, and evaluate on future windows. This would test whether online DQN refinement remains useful when graph drift, demand drift, and perturbation are observed rather than sampled.

Third, the validation gate should be calibrated rather than treated as a contribution by itself. The final sweep shows that the gate selects Online DQN in all six perturbation windows, and at \(p=0.25\) the keep-current row is slightly better on observed mean objective. Future work should add confidence intervals, sequential statistical tests, and explicit no-op thresholds so that the controller can avoid cache rewriting when the expected gain is not statistically reliable.

Fourth, the system should be scaled beyond 20 APs and 48 content objects. Larger evaluations will require batched shortest-path computation, more efficient marginal-gain screening, and possibly distributed training. The online DQN action space also grows with \(N|\mathcal{C}|\), so larger deployments may need hierarchical actions, candidate pruning, or graph-based action embeddings.

Finally, graph neural policies are a natural next step. A GNN could replace explicit degree, weighted-degree, and closeness features with a permutation-aware encoder that generalizes across topologies. Such a model should still be evaluated against diversified placement, topology-aware greedy, keep-current, and transition-aware Online DQN, and it should continue to charge relocation cost against the previous deployed cache.
